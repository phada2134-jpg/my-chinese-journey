📍 Where It All Began

My Background

🏛️ Angkor Wat — Siem Reap Province, my birthplace
🌿 Tbong Khmum Province (特本克蒙省) — My Hometown

One month after I was born in Siem Reap, my parents moved back to their hometown in Tbong Khmum Province (特本克蒙省). I am a single child from a poor family in a rural area. My parents are strict but always want to give me the best education.

They sent me to English school when I was only four years old. Later, when I was in Grade 4 in Khmer school, my mother sent me to Chinese school starting from Grade 1. I never expected to learn Chinese — my mother simply sent me there and I followed her decision.

👵 Raised with Love

Because my family was poor, my parents worked very hard and sometimes could not return home. The people who raised me were my grandmother (now 82 years old) and my single aunt (now 52 years old). They gave me warmth, food, encouragement, and taught me morality and how to be a good person. Living far from my parents, I learned to be independent.

"Do not avoid work. Whatever you can do, you should do it.
Do not think about whether it is a boy's job or a girl's job."

— My Mother's Words

🌱 2015 — The Start of Everything

The Beginning of My Chinese Journey

🏫 蓬坡社公立华侨学校 — Where My Chinese Journey Began (2015–2023)

🏫 蓬坡社公立华侨学校

My Chinese learning journey started here. I studied from Grade 1 to Grade 6 between 2015 and 2022 (with a COVID interruption). At the beginning I had no interest in Chinese, but as I studied day by day I slowly began to enjoy it. I studied three languages every day: Khmer, Chinese, and English.

💬 What People Said

Some people asked my parents: "Are you afraid your child will become crazy from studying too much?"

My mother answered: "I trust my son. He will be able to absorb everything he learns."

⏸️ Grade 11 & 12 — A Difficult Trade-off

Gap Years

During Grade 11 and Grade 12, I focused only on Khmer studies to achieve Grade A in the Baccalaureate exam. Because I did not practice Chinese much during those two years, my Chinese ability became weaker. This was a difficult trade-off — but a necessary sacrifice for my academic future.

Sometimes we must pause to prepare for a greater leap forward.

🌆 2024 — A New Chapter in the Capital

Life in Phnom Penh

🏆 Grade A Baccalaureate — Me & My Mom
🎓 Institut de Technologie du Cambodge (ITC) — 柬埔寨理工大学

After graduating from high school in 2024 with Grade A, I moved to Phnom Penh to study at the Institution of Technology of Cambodia (柬埔寨理工大学), studying Software Engineering — while also searching for a Chinese school to continue improving my Chinese.

🏫 2024–2025 — The Turning Point

CAM-ASEAN Showed Me the Path

📋 The Scholarship Opportunity

Because I achieved Grade A, CAM-ASEAN International Institute contacted me and offered a one-year scholarship. To enroll I had to pass: 阅读 (Reading) and 面试 (Interview). My reading score was 64/70. Because my speaking was weaker at that time, I was placed in GCP4.

📈 My Journey: GCP4 → GCP8

I studied for almost one year, progressing from GCP4 all the way to GCP8. At the beginning I did not know anyone, but as time passed my classmates became very friendly and supportive, and the classroom atmosphere became very warm.

📚 How to Succeed in Chinese

Effective Methods for Learning Chinese

✍️ Chinese Calligraphy — Mastering Every Stroke
每天练习
Practice Every Day
📅

Practice Daily

Consistency beats intensity. Even 30 minutes daily builds strong habits and deep retention.

🔁

Review Old Lessons

Regularly revisiting material reinforces memory and prevents forgetting vocabulary and grammar.

🗣️

Speak with Natives

Conversations with native speakers improve pronunciation, natural flow, and cultural expression.

📖

Read Chinese Texts

Reading books, articles, and news builds vocabulary in context and boosts comprehension.

💪

Embrace Mistakes

Don't be afraid to make errors. Every mistake is a lesson. Speak boldly and learn from corrections.

✍️

Write Characters

Handwriting 汉字 every day builds muscle memory, improves stroke order, and deepens understanding.
